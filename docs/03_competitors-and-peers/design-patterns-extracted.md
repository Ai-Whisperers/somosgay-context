# Design patterns to extract

> **TL;DR:** 14 patterns worth borrowing for the SOMOSGAY rebuild, drawn from Whitman-Walker, LA LGBT Center, Fenway Health, Callen-Lorde, DIKÉ, CenterLink.

---

## Patterns

### 1. "Become a Patient" / "Become a Member" prominent CTA

Every clinic site has this in the top-right of the nav. SOMOSGAY doesn't. **Add it.**

### 2. Multi-location finder

Whitman-Walker (multiple DC sites), Callen-Lorde (Chelsea + Brooklyn + Bronx), Fenway (multiple locations). SOMOSGAY has one physical location but could surface partner pickup points (barbería Los Barberos de López, sauna Rebellion) the same way.

### 3. Service grid in main nav (4–6 columns)

LA LGBT Center's main nav has services broken into columns: Youth / Senior / Trans / Survivor / Medical / Mental Health / Sexual Health / Substance Use / Legal / Housing. Same model fits SOMOSGAY: Clínica Kunu'u / Tekoharã / Ñande Rekorã / Karu Porã / Programa Kunu'u / Pride-Besatón / Youth Shelter.

### 4. Real numbers on the homepage

LA LGBT Center: "500,000 Visits per Year · 800+ Team Members · 117,286 Meals Prepared · 55,000+ Medical Visits per Year." Whitman-Walker: "20,000 patients/year." DIKÉ: "150 patients on hormones." SOMOSGAY's current site shows zero concrete numbers. **Add them.**

### 5. Same-day appointment as a feature

Callen-Lorde's "Quick Care" + Whitman-Walker's "SMART Immediate Care" both market same-day walk-in. SOMOSGAY's Mon–Fri 13:00–17:00 testing + Sat autotest is the same pattern. **Make it visible.**

### 6. Multilingual access page

Fenway's interpretation services page lists English / Spanish / Portuguese / Chinese / Haitian Creole. SOMOSGAY should have at minimum Spanish (primary), English (international donors/press), and ideally Guaraní (cultural authenticity).

### 7. Name + gender marker change resources

Fenway has a Massachusetts-specific resource. SOMOSGAY could do a Paraguay-specific one (even if there's no legal framework, social practice is real — Paloma Vera mentions this).

### 8. Free legal services page

Whitman-Walker's legal services are explicitly FREE. SOMOSGAY's GlobalGiving campaign is for legal aid + psychological support — make it visible.

### 9. Patient/client stories

LA LGBT Center has "Client Stories." SOMOSGAY should collect anonymized stories (clinic patient, youth-shelter resident, Marcha del Orgullo attendee).

### 10. Strategic roadmap transparency

Fenway's "Forward Together" page publicly shares the strategic plan. SOMOSGAY could publish its annual plan.

### 11. Active blog / news / events calendar

All seven benchmark sites have this. SOMOSGAY has /news but no events calendar. **Add events calendar.**

### 12. Multiple ways to engage

LA LGBT Center: Donate / Volunteer / Lifeworks Mentorship / Corporate Engagement / Make a Legacy Gift / Spaces. SOMOSGAY should have at minimum: Donate / Volunteer / Hire / Partner.

### 13. Donor transparency / annual report

Whitman-Walker publishes 50+ year legacy + research participation rate. SOMOSGAY already publishes audit + annual reports — surface them prominently on the new site.

### 14. Resilience messaging

Callen-Lorde's "We're Here for You. Always." during hostile political climate. SOMOSGAY's current site has nothing like this — could add a strong closing statement: "Defendemos derechos. Construimos comunidad. Desde 2005."

---

## Anti-patterns to avoid

### ❌ Hidden donation CTA

Some sites bury donation. SOMOSGAY already has GlobalGiving + All Out — make them visible.

### ❌ Vague "Services" page

Some sites list "Medical" without specifying. SOMOSGAY should spell out: HIV test, PrEP, TARV, syphilis, Hep B, psychology, psychiatry, harm reduction.

### ❌ Carousel-heavy homepage

LA LGBT Center has multiple carousels — slow + bad SEO. SOMOSGAY should prefer static sections.

### ❌ Multiple popups

LA LGBT Center has multiple "Donate" overlays. Annoying. SOMOSGAY should use one persistent top banner + footer CTA only.

### ❌ No contact info

Most SOMOSGAY competitors (AIREANA, Panambi) bury contact. SOMOSGAY's site has footer phone/email — keep this.

## Visual identity patterns

- **Rainbow as accent, not wallpaper** — LA LGBT Center uses rainbow sparingly; The Center (NYC) uses it on logo only
- **Photos of real people, not stock** — Whitman-Walker, Fenway use real patient photos (with consent)
- **High-contrast typography** — every benchmark site has clear type hierarchy
- **Generous whitespace** — Fenway, Whitman-Walker have air; SOMOSGAY's current site is cramped

---

## Mobile patterns

- **Click-to-call** prominent in mobile nav — most do this; SOMOSGAY already has +595 986 173 200
- **WhatsApp button** floating — standard for LATAM; SOMOSGAY already uses WhatsApp for booking
- **Quick-links menu** — most have hamburger with 4–6 quick actions
