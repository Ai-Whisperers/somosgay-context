## Etymology notes

### Tekoporã

- Guaraní philosophical concept
- Rough translation: "good living" or "buen vivir"
- Related to the Andean "sumak kawsay" / Quechua "sumaq qaway"
- Used by Paraguayan + Bolivian + Argentinian Guaraní communities
- Frames social justice as rooted in indigenous philosophy, not Western liberal concepts

### Tekoha

- The Guaraní concept of a community's place of being
- Includes land, social structure, language, spirituality
- Used by Guaraní land-rights movements in Paraguay
- Likely the root of "Tekoharã" (community-centre) — community-as-place

### Kunumi / Kunu'u

- "Kunumi" = boy / young man in Guaraní
- "Kunu'u" — possibly a derived or related form, possibly a proper name
- We have NOT confirmed the official translation from the org

## How to use Guaraní terms in the rebuild

1. **Always preserve accents.** Tekoporã not Tekopora. Karu Porã not Karu Pora.
2. **Use Guaraní as primary name, Spanish as secondary.** "Clínica Kunu'u — antes Kuimba'e" not "Clínica Kuimba'e — ahora Kunu'u" (when referencing the rebranded current name).
3. **Don't translate program names in headlines.** Use the Guaraní name, then explain on hover/click.
4. **Add a Spanish glossary page.** "Glosario de términos" — for site visitors unfamiliar with Guaraní terms.
5. **Use the etymological context where it matters.** On a "Tekoharã" page, explain the Guaraní concept of *tekoha* and why it matters.
6. **Don't overdo it.** The current public-facing material uses Guaraní names without translation in headlines. Mirror this.

## Spanish/Guaraní code-switching patterns in their existing copy

Examples from their own text:

- *"Defender el avance de los derechos humanos de gays, lesbianas, transgéneros, bisexuales y sus familias"* (Spanish-only)
- *"En el marco del programa KUNU'U para promover el cuidado, la salud, el placer y el bienestar con énfasis en hombres gays y bisexuales"* (Spanish with KUNU'U as proper noun)
- *"Centro Comunitario TEKOHARÃ"* (Spanish label with Guaraní proper noun)
- *"Sistema de cuidado comunitario Ñande Rekorã"* (Spanish label with Guaraní proper noun)
- *"Yo <3 PrEP en Paraguay: Yo <3 Más Seguro"* (Spanish + emoji)

Pattern: Spanish is the working language; Guaraní is used for proper nouns + framing concepts (tekoporã).

## Cultural sensitivity rules

- **Never use Guaraní as a marketing gimmick.** It carries real cultural weight.
- **Don't invent terms.** If you need a new term, work with the org (ideally with a Guaraní-speaking team member or community partner).
- **Preserve the Guaraní orthography** — Paraguay uses the "Jopara" standard (mixed Guaraní-Spanish)
- **Cite sources** when introducing cultural concepts — link to the page that explains it
