# Uso de estandartes — when to use what

> **TL;DR:** Three core visual markers: (1) the rainbow flag, (2) the "108" mark, (3) the Tekoporã frame. Each has rules.

---

## The rainbow flag

### When to use

- ✅ Pride + LGBTQ+ visibility contexts
- ✅ Default "we are LGBTQ+ org" signal
- ✅ Youth programming
- ✅ International LGBTQ+ events (IDAHOBIT, World AIDS Day)

### When NOT to use

- ❌ On documents that may be presented to hostile government officials (some clinics avoid it on clinical forms to reduce friction)
- ❌ As background wallpaper (cheap, dilutes the meaning)
- ❌ Over-used (preserve meaning by using sparingly)

### Variations

- **Classic 6-stripe rainbow** — most universal, safest
- **Progress Pride flag** (Daniel Quasar 2018) — adds trans + POC stripes; some orgs have adopted
- **[VERIFY]** which version SOMOSGAY currently uses

## The "108" mark

### When to use

- ✅ Memorial contexts (anniversary of 1959 raids)
- ✅ Youth shelter materials (the shelter has 108-tiled fountain)
- ✅ Political advocacy materials (marriage equality, education ban)
- ✅ Solidarity statements

### When NOT to use

- ❌ As decorative element on a clinical form
- ❌ On donation receipts (could cause donor concern in hostile contexts)
- ❌ In contexts where it could be misinterpreted as numerical spam or, worse, as a target

### Always pair with context

> *"108 — En 1959, 108 personas fueron detenidas y torturadas bajo la dictadura de Stroessner. El número 108 es un emblema de resistencia para la comunidad LGBT en Paraguay."*

(Translation: 108 — In 1959, 108 people were detained and tortured under Stroessner's dictatorship. The number 108 is an emblem of resistance for the LGBT community in Paraguay.)

### Don't

- ❌ Don't use 108 alone without context — it loses its meaning
- ❌ Don't make it look like a logo — it's a memorial mark
- ❌ Don't commercialize it

## The Tekoporã frame

### When to use

- ✅ Mission statements
- ✅ "About us" pages
- ✅ Closing statements on reports
- ✅ Slogan / value statement on the homepage
- ✅ Donor pitches (frames the work as Paraguayan-rooted)

### When NOT to use

- ❌ On clinical documents (use plain Spanish)
- ❌ As a tagline on every page (loses impact)

### Suggested taglines

- *"Construyendo el tekoporã desde 2005"* (Building tekoporã since 2005)
- *"Tekoporã para todes"* (Good living for everyone — note: "todes" is the Paraguayan/LATAM gender-neutral pronoun)
- *"Por el tekoporã de nuestra comunidad"* (For the tekoporã of our community)

---

## Visual hierarchy

| Asset | Weight | Position |
|---|---|---|
| SOMOSGAY logo | Primary | Top-left, every page |
| Rainbow | Secondary accent | Nav, dividers, occasional CTA |
| 108 mark | Memorial | Anniversary dates, advocacy, shelter |
| Tekoporã framing | Voice | Mission, about, value pages |
| Paraguayan flag (red-white-blue) | Optional | When asserting Paraguayan identity vs foreign framing |

---

## Combined usage example

A homepage hero could combine:
- SOMOSGAY logo (top-left)
- Defiant headline in Spanish
- Subhead with tekoporã reference
- Rainbow accent in CTA button
- 108 mark only if the page references the historical context

Example:
> **"Defendemos derechos. Cuidamos comunidad. Desde 2005."**
> *Construyendo el tekoporã para la comunidad LGBT en Paraguay.*
