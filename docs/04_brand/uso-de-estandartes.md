## The "108" — verified history

### Origin: September 1959

- **Bernardo Aranda Valdez**, 25-year-old radio announcer at **Radio Comuneros**
- Found burned to death in his bed on **1 September 1959**, in his home at Estados Unidos y Novena Proyectadas streets, Barrio Obrero, Asunción
- Police declared it a "crime of passion"
- Public pressure mounted for an investigation
- Police used the case as pretext to raid Asunción's gay community

### The 10-day raids (Sept 2-12, 1959)

- **Sept 2:** Police began violent + arbitrary arrests of suspected homosexuals
- **Sept 7:** Public statement that those arrested had "dubious sexual conduct"
- **Sept 11:** Newspaper **El País** (Stroessner-aligned) published the headline: *"108 People of Dubious Moral Conduct Are Being Interrogated. Intense Police Action. Results Expected."* — **The number 108 first appears here**
- **108 was NOT an official figure** — it was a journalist's count from a list of arrested suspects
- Over Sept 12-30, raids continued; raids extended for **10 years** through the dictatorship

### The "Carta de un Amoral" — pre-Stonewall

- **30 September 1959** (3 days after the raids' first round): Anonymous letter published in El País titled *"Carta de un Amoral"* (Letter from an Immoral Man)
- **Claimed as the first public demonstration for sexual freedom in Paraguay**
- **Predates the Stonewall riots (1969) by 10 years**

### Memory work

- **2010:** Documentary *"108 Cuchillo de Palo"* by Paraguayan filmmaker **Renate Costa** (about her uncle, one of the 108)
- **2013:** Lawyer + LGBT activist **Erwing Augsten Szokol** published book *"108 Ciento Ocho"* (ISBN 978-99953-2-740-8)
- **AIREANA** commemorates September as the month of 108 memories
- SOMOSGAY uses 108 in visual identity (youth shelter fountain has 108 rainbow tiles)

## The 108 mark — usage rules

### When to use

- ✅ Memorial contexts (anniversary of 1959 raids — September is the month)
- ✅ Youth shelter materials (the shelter has 108-tiled fountain)
- ✅ Political advocacy materials (marriage equality, education ban)
- ✅ Solidarity statements
- ✅ Annual **Mes de las Memorias 108** (September commemorations)

### When NOT to use

- ❌ As decorative element on a clinical form
- ❌ On donation receipts (could cause donor concern in hostile contexts)
- ❌ In contexts where it could be misinterpreted
- ❌ As a logo (it's a memorial mark)

### Always pair with context

> *"108 — En septiembre de 1959, 108 personas fueron detenidas y torturadas bajo la dictadura de Stroessner tras el asesinato del locutor Bernardo Aranda. El número 108 es hoy un emblema de resistencia para la comunidad LGBT en Paraguay."*

(Translation: 108 — In September 1959, 108 people were detained and tortured under Stroessner's dictatorship following the murder of radio announcer Bernardo Aranda. The number 108 is today an emblem of resistance for the LGBT community in Paraguay.)

### Don't

- ❌ Don't use 108 alone without context — it loses its meaning
- ❌ Don't make it look like a logo — it's a memorial mark
- ❌ Don't commercialize it

## The rainbow flag

### When to use

- ✅ Pride + LGBTQ+ visibility contexts
- ✅ Default "we are LGBTQ+ org" signal
- ✅ Youth programming
- ✅ International LGBTQ+ events (IDAHOBIT, World AIDS Day)

### When NOT to use

- ❌ On documents that may be presented to hostile government officials
- ❌ As background wallpaper (cheap, dilutes the meaning)
- ❌ Over-used (preserve meaning by using sparingly)

### Variations

- **Classic 6-stripe rainbow** — most universal, safest
- **Progress Pride flag** (Daniel Quasar 2018) — adds trans + POC stripes; some orgs have adopted

## The Tekoporã frame

### When to use

- ✅ Mission statements
- ✅ "About us" pages
- ✅ Closing statements on reports
- ✅ Slogan / value statement on the homepage
- ✅ Donor pitches (frames the work as Paraguayan-rooted)

### When NOT to use

- ❌ On clinical documents (use plain Spanish)
- ❌ As a tagline on every page (loses impact)

### Suggested taglines

- *"Construyendo el tekoporã desde 2005"* (Building tekoporã since 2005)
- *"Tekoporã para todes"* (Good living for everyone — note: "todes" is the Paraguayan/LATAM gender-neutral pronoun)
- *"Por el tekoporã de nuestra comunidad"* (For the tekoporã of our community)
