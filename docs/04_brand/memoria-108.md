> **TL;DR:** The **Caso 108** is the foundational trauma of the Paraguayan LGBTQ+ movement. Bernardo Aranda, a 25-year-old radio announcer, was murdered on **1 September 1959**. Police used his death as pretext for **10 days of raids** that tortured 108+ gay men. The number 108 was coined by a journalist, not by the state. The 108's anonymous response, *"Carta de un Amoral"* (30 Sept 1959), predates Stonewall by 10 years.

## The night of 1 September 1959

### Bernardo Aranda Valdez

- **Age:** 25
- **Occupation:** Radio announcer at **Radio Comuneros**
- **Also:** Dancer (per Wikipedia ES)
- **Address:** Estados Unidos y Novena Proyectadas, **Barrio Obrero**, Asunción
- **Coordinates:** 25°18′06″S 57°38′11″O
- **Date of death:** 1 September 1959, ~02:00 AM
- **Discovery:** Body found burned in his bed
- **Police ruling:** "Crime of passion" / "asfixia por sofocación debido a los gases en combustión" (asphyxiation by combustion gases)
- **Case status:** Never solved

### Why it mattered

- Aranda was **popular** — public pressure to solve the murder
- Stroessner dictatorship (1954-1989) was in early years
- Police used the case as pretext for broader gay community persecution

## The 10-day raids (Sept 2-12, 1959)

| Date | Event |
|---|---|
| **Sept 2** | Police begin violent + arbitrary arrests of suspected homosexuals |
| **Sept 7** | First public statement that those arrested had "dubious sexual conduct" (intent: link to Aranda murder) |
| **Sept 11 or 12** | **El País** newspaper publishes headline: **"108 People of Dubious Moral Conduct Are Being Interrogated. Intense Police Action. Results Expected."** — the number 108 first appears |
| **Sept 17** | Autopsy result published: "asphyxiation by combustion gases" |
| **Sept 21** | Major police raid on gay community gathering places |
| **Sept 23** | Leaflets distributed naming 43 "immorals" (signed "Parents' Committee, for the cleansing of our society") |
| **Sept 30** | **"Carta de un Amoral"** (Letter from an Immoral Man) published in El País — pre-Stonewall response |

**The raids extended for 10 years through the dictatorship.**

---

## Why the number 108 became iconic

- **NOT an official figure** — it was a journalist's count from a list
- The journalist (not the state) chose to publish "108" — that became the lasting symbol
- The number was then used pejoratively for years
- LGBTQ+ orgs reclaimed it as a symbol of resistance
- SOMOSGAY uses it in visual identity (youth shelter fountain has 108 rainbow tiles)

---

## "Carta de un Amoral" — pre-Stonewall by 10 years

- **Date:** 30 September 1959
- **Author:** Anonymous
- **Published in:** El País
- **Significance:** First public demonstration for sexual freedom in Paraguay
- **Predates Stonewall (1969) by 10 years**

This letter is claimed by Paraguayan LGBTQ+ orgs as a foundational document of the movement.

## Memory work (post-dictatorship)

### 2010 — Documentary "108 Cuchillo de Palo"

- **Director:** Renate Costa (Paraguayan filmmaker)
- **Subject:** Her uncle, one of the 108
- **Significance:** Brought the case to international cinema audience

### 2013 — Book "108 Ciento Ocho"

- **Author:** Erwing Augsten Szokol (lawyer + LGBT activist)
- **Publisher:** Arandura, Asunción
- **ISBN:** 978-99953-2-740-8
- **Significance:** Definitive historical + legal research on the case
- **Source:** https://es.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caso_108

### Annual commemoration

- **September** is the **Mes de las Memorias 108** (Month of 108 Memories) in Paraguay
- AIREANA + SOMOSGAY co-organize commemorative events
- 1 September = anniversary of Aranda's death
- 30 September = anniversary of "Carta de un Amoral"
