# Clínica Kunu'u

> **TL;DR:** SOMOSGAY's flagship community clinic. Free HIV testing, free PrEP, free syphilis + hepatitis B screening, free TARV treatment, free psychology + psychiatry (incl. remote), harm-reduction services, autotest pickup. Mon–Fri 13:00–17:00 testing, Sat 10:00–15:00 autotest pickup. Coordinator: Ramón Martínez. Family physician: Dr. Carlos Santa María. Address: Independencia Nacional 1032, Asunción. Originally launched as **Kuimba'e** in 2013; rebranded to **Kunu'u** later.

---

## Name & etymology

- **Kunu'u** — Guaraní. We have not found a published translation; **[VERIFY]**. Likely related to "kunumi" (boy/young man) or similar.
- The clinic was originally called **Kuimba'e** when it opened in May 2013 ("kuimba'e" = "men" in Guaraní per Washington Blade 2012 announcement).
- Rebrand to Kunu'u appears to have happened sometime between 2013 and 2023.

## Origin: Kuimba'e (2013)

Washington Blade (Dec 2012):
> *"Somosgay hopes to offer free rapid HIV/AIDS testing to those who access services at the clinic that will be called Kuimba'e, which means men in the local Guaraní language, in Asunción, the country's capital. Simón Cazal, the group's chief executive officer, told the Washington Blade during an interview on Monday that he plans to invite proctologists and specialists in anal and rectal health to work in the facility."*

> *"This initiative from Somosgay will establish a community clinic that will specialize in men's health and well-being," Somosgay said in a Nov. 26 press release. "The doors of this clinic will open in 2013 in the center of Asunción. In addition to this project, the initiative plans to further expand voluntary testing and access to anti-HIV treatment in Paraguay through specific campaigns aimed at the entire population."*

> *"19 percent of gay men and 29 percent of transgender women in the country live with HIV. Cazal noted Paraguayan public health officials offered only 80,000 HIV tests — 72,000 to pregnant women and 8,000 to men and trans women — last year."*

> *"The Los Angeles-based AIDS Healthcare Foundation, amfAR and the Fundación Triángulo (Triangle Foundation) in Spain are among the organizations backing the Somosgay clinic."*

## Public health context (Paraguay HIV)

- **2022:** >1,500 new HIV cases diagnosed in Paraguay
- **2020→2022:** +26.48% increase in new diagnoses (vs pandemic era)
- **Vulnerable populations:** young gay/bisexual men + trans women (per SOMOSGAY quoting MSPBS/PRONASIDA)
- **PrEP efficacy:** 99% when taken daily
- Source: https://www.abc.com.py/nacionales/2023/11/20/lanzan-campana-del-dia-de-la-respuesta-al-vih-yo-amo-prep-yo-amo-mas-seguro/

## What this needs from the new website

1. **Booking/appointment flow** — currently WhatsApp-only; a real appointment calendar would reduce no-shows and double-bookings
2. **Multilingual service descriptions** — at minimum Spanish, ideally English for international donors and Guaraní for local outreach
3. **Map + pickup points** for autotest (current partner barbershops + sauna)
4. **"What to expect" content** for first-time visitors — many Paraguayan LGBTQ+ people have never been to a clinic before; a clear "this is what happens when you arrive" page reduces fear
5. **Tele-psychiatry page** for the remote psychiatry service
6. **Press / impact dashboard** — patient counts per year, tests administered, PrEP enrollments (current site has zero)
7. **Public health resources** — PrEP FAQ, autotest instructions (downloadable PDF), harm-reduction guides
8. **Donation CTA** specifically for the clinic
9. **Volunteer / hire CTA** for clinical + non-clinical roles
10. **Compliance + transparency** — link to the public audit reports
11. **Privacy + safety notice** — given political climate, make clear what data is collected and how
