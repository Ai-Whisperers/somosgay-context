# Historia y fundación

> **TL;DR:** Origins in 2005, formalised 23 May 2009, in response to violence against LGBTQ+ people in Paraguay's post-dictatorship transition. Co-founded by Simón Cazal + others (full co-founder list not public). First major win: 2009 cooperation agreement with Ministry of Interior. First clinic: HIV testing centre 2012, men's health clinic (Kuimba'e) 2013, rebranded to Clínica Kunu'u.

---

## Origin story

From the org's own /about page (https://www.somosgay.org/about):

> *"SOMOSGAY se origina en 2005 y es formalizada el 23 de mayo de 2009 como una respuesta solidaria hacia la violencia ejercida hacia las personas LGTBI+ en Paraguay, durante la inestable transición democrática tras una de las más largas dictaduras militares de la región."*

Translation: *"SOMOSGAY originated in 2005 and was formalised on 23 May 2009 as a solidarity response to violence against LGBTI+ people in Paraguay, during the unstable democratic transition following one of the longest military dictatorships in the region."*

Frames itself around the Guaraní concept of **tekoporã** ("buen vivir" = good living):
> *"Nuestra organización marca su compromiso con la construcción de una sociedad libre de opresiones, plenamente igualitaria, reivindicando nuestra herencia guaraní que aspira a lograr el tekoporã (buen vivir) para todes."*

The "108" reference (the 1959 police raids that tortured 108 alleged gay men under Stroessner) is woven through their visual identity (the youth-shelter fountain, publications, and political messaging).

## Key political milestones (national context)

- **1992 Constitution** — technically egalitarian, but interpretation consistently blocks marriage equality
- **2018 CIDH ruling** — Inter-American Commission rules that family ties can derive from same-sex relationships; Paraguay ignores (it ratified the Convention in 1989 and accepted CIDH jurisdiction in 1993)
- **2017 Education Ban** — Resolution 29664 still in force; prohibits any discussion of gender/sexuality in public schools
- **2024 Marriage Equality** — still not in Paraguay; the only South American country without it; sits behind Argentina, Brazil, Uruguay, Colombia, Chile (2022), Ecuador
