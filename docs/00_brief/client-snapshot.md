## The five Ws

**Who** — A 17-year-old LGBTQ+ rights + community health organisation. Run by ~10–25 paid staff plus volunteers, co-led since at least 2023 by Executive Director **Paloma Vera**, with co-founder **Simón Cazal** as the long-tenured public face and international spokesperson. **Ramón Martínez** coordinates Clínica Kunu'u. **Dr. Carlos Santa María** is Kunu'u's family physician. Other named staff in press: Dr. Carlos (medical lead, TikTok feature 2023), Red Cross partner for first-aid training (2024).

**What** — Five integrated programs + the clinic + the app:
1. **Clínica Kunu'u** — free HIV testing, PrEP, TARV, syphilis + hepatitis B screening, general medical, psychology + psychiatry (incl. remote), harm reduction
2. **Centro Comunitario Tekoharã** — community centre for workshops, talks, testing events
3. **Ñande Rekorã** — community care system
4. **Karu Porã** — healthy-food program (DNIT donated supplies Apr 2024)
5. **Programa Kunu'u** — public-health campaigns ("Yo ❤️ PrEP: Yo Amo Más Seguro", "Comunidad en Prevención Combinada" mental-health)
6. **Equis** — Android app for clinic booking + info (2014)
7. Annual **Besatón** (kiss-in at Parliament) + **Marcha del Orgullo** (Asunción Pride)
8. Youth shelter ~40 min outside Asunción (capacity ~5 teens + chaperone, "108-tiled" rainbow courtyard)

**Where** — One physical hub in central Asunción (Independencia Nacional 1032) + youth shelter 40 min outside the city. PrEP also available through 9 partner public hospitals: Asunción (Barrio Obrero, PRONASIDA, Materno Infantil San Pablo), plus Capiatá, Encarnación, Ciudad del Este, Pedro Juan Caballero, Villa Elisa.

**When** — Active since 2009. Major milestones:
- 2009 — founded, signed cooperation with Ministry of Interior
- 2012 — opened first HIV testing centre
- 2013 — opened **Kuimba'e** men's health clinic (rebranded **Kunu'u** later)
- 2012 — Simón Cazal married Sergio López in Rosario, Argentina
- 2013 — Judge Nery Kunzle refuses marriage recognition in Paraguay
- 2014 — Equis app launched
- 2015 — Cazal met Pope Francis; Red GayLatino co-founded
- 2017 — Ministry of Education resolution 29664 bans "gender ideology" materials
- 2020–2022 — pandemic continuity; HIV diagnoses up 26.48%
- 2023 — Paloma Vera is ED; "Yo ❤️ PrEP" campaign
- 2024 — Red Cross first-aid training; DNIT food donation; LA Times/ABC Color international coverage
- 2026 — engagement scout by Ai-Whisperers (this repo)

**Why we care** —
1. Massive gap between what they *do* and what their *website* shows
2. Real public-health stakes (PrEP access, HIV prevention, LGBTIfobia reporting)
3. Profile fits Ai-Whisperers' "sensitive Paraguay verticals" portfolio (dentist, sarah-lubricants, pierce-charm)
4. Existing public docs available for review (audit reports, annual reports, anti-corruption policy) — suggests they value transparency
5. Direct donor relationships + international press footprint = recurring revenue potential for ongoing engagement
