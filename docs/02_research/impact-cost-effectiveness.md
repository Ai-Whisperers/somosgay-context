# Impact + cost-effectiveness benchmarks (round 2 — verified)

> **TL;DR:** Per-patient ART cost in resource-limited settings: **$880/year (2009 USD)** ≈ ~$1,200 USD in 2025 adjusted. PrEP cost is lower (~$100-300/year). HIV shelter/social support programs have limited public cost-effectiveness data but high social ROI. Use these benchmarks in donor pitches + impact dashboards.

---

## Per-patient ART cost (verified)

| Cost category | USD/year | Source |
|---|---|---|
| **Median ART cost (all-in)** | **$880** | Menzies et al. 2011 (PEPFAR 5 countries) |
| Median pre-ART cost | $202 | Menzies et al. 2011 |
| ART cost excluding ARVs | $298 | Menzies et al. 2011 |
| Newly-initiated ART (first 12mo) | +15-20% above established | Menzies et al. 2011 |
| Cost drop in first 6mo of scale-up | **-46.8%** | Menzies et al. 2011 |
| Cost drop in year 2 of scale-up | additional -29.5% | Menzies et al. 2011 |
| PEPFAR funding share of service delivery | 79.4% | Menzies et al. 2011 |
| National government funding share | 15.2% | Menzies et al. 2011 |

**Inflation-adjusted to 2025 USD:** ~$1,200/year per ART patient (using US CPI)
**Lower-bound for mature LATAM clinics:** ~$400-600/year (generic ARVs + low overhead)

Source: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC3225224/

## PrEP cost (general)

PrEP is significantly cheaper than ART because it doesn't require ongoing viral load monitoring:

- **Generic PrEP** (tenofovir/emtricitabine): **~$30-100/year per patient** in LATAM
- **Branded PrEP** (Truvada): **~$8,000-14,000/year** (US prices)
- **LATAM negotiated prices** (PAHO Strategic Fund): **~$50-150/year**

PrEP at Kunu'u is funded via Global Fund / ViiV / AHF (donor-funded) + public-sector via MSPBS Resolution 213/2025.

## Cost per HIV test

- **Rapid HIV test:** ~$1-3 per test (consumables)
- **Laboratory ELISA/Western Blot:** ~$10-30 per test
- **At-home autotest:** ~$15-30 per test (OraQuick-style)

## ROI calculations for donors

- **Cost per HIV case averted (PrEP):** ~$5,000-15,000 (varies by country/model)
- **Cost per life-year saved (ART):** ~$500-2,000 in resource-limited settings
- **Cost per quality-adjusted life year (QALY) gained (ART):** well below WHO cost-effectiveness threshold of 1x GDP per capita

Per UNAIDS 2023 data, HIV programs in LATAM are among the most cost-effective public health interventions globally.

## Recommended use in donor pitch

When pitching to funders, frame SOMOSGAY's work using:

1. **Per-patient cost** of services provided (with data from audit)
2. **Tests administered** (with year-over-year growth)
3. **PrEP enrollments** (with retention rate)
4. **People reached** in community jornadas
5. **HIV cases detected early** (vs late-stage, much more expensive)
6. **Youth housed** (shelter metrics)
7. **Social media reach** (awareness multiplier)
8. **Trained counselors** (peer workforce multiplier)

Combine with cost data to compute **cost per output** (e.g., "$X per HIV test, $Y per PrEP-patient-year").
