> **TL;DR:** Crypto donations are technically feasible via **The Giving Block** (US-based, $0 setup for nonprofits). But for Paraguay: regulatory uncertainty + donor demographic (likely 99%+ traditional banking) + OPSEC risks make this **a v3 nice-to-have, not a v1 priority**.

## Paraguay context

- Paraguay has a **basic regulatory framework** for crypto (not banned, not fully regulated)
- Bitso + other exchanges operate
- Crypto adoption growing but not mainstream
- Donor base for SOMOSGAY likely <1% crypto-native

---

## Pros of accepting crypto for SOMOSGAY

- Borderless donations (US/EU donors can give without bank wires)
- Lower transaction fees than bank wires (especially for international)
- Some crypto donors specifically seek out nonprofits to donate to
- Public blockchain = transparency
- Useful for political resistance fundraising (harder to seize than bank accounts)

## Cons of accepting crypto for SOMOSGAY

- Regulatory uncertainty in PY
- Volatility risk (donation value can swing 20% in a week)
- OPSEC risk: blockchain is public — donations are traceable
- Donor demographic likely doesn't have crypto
- Complexity for staff (need wallet management, accounting for crypto assets)
- Conversion to PYG requires exchange (with fees + tax reporting)

---

## Recommendation

**Do not add crypto donations in v1 of the rebuild.**

Reasons:
- 99%+ of donors use traditional banking
- OPSEC risk
- Complexity not worth the tiny marginal benefit
- Can add later if a specific donor asks

If SOMOSGAY decides they want crypto later:
- Use **The Giving Block** (handles conversion to USD)
- Or self-hosted BTCPay Server (more control, more complexity)
